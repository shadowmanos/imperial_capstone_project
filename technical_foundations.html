Technical foundations of your black-box optimisation challenge

  • What is the main technical justification for your current BBO approach? Which aspect of prior research or established methods supports your choice?

    I am using Bayesian Optimisation with a Gaussian Process as a surrogate model. GP is reported on many online sources as appropriate when few samples are available e.g. fewer than a few 1000s and we have 10-50 hear, and inappropriate for more than a few dozen dimensions, and we have max 8 here. BO has been recommended in this course and other sources for black box optimisations with expensive functions to evaluate.

  • Which academic papers have you used to guide your design? Which ideas or techniques from the literature are most relevant, and how do they strengthen your project?

    I've mostly followed the documentation for scikit-learn and scipy rather than academic papers. I also googled examples for BO with GP to come up with a decent python script. I am trying different kernels and different hyperparameters monitoring which yields good improvement for each of the 8 black box functions. My approach is more practical than academic.

  • Which third-party libraries or frameworks (e.g. PyTorch, TensorFlow, scikit-learn) are central to your approach? Why were these the right choices compared with possible alternatives?

    I am primarily relying on scikit-learn for the Gaussian Process and kernels, and scipy for the optimisation functionality. These are well known libraries, widely used in machine learning in general, and in this course in particular. They are rather easy to use by people like me that don't have a PhD on the subject and are more practical than academic. Documentation is good and there are many tutorials and blogs to steal ideas from. Python is far more practical to use than equivalent C/C++ programs than need compiling. I've avoided neural networks for surrogate models as the advice I've seen online is that they work better for more than a few 1000 samples. Consequently, I haven't touched Pytorch or Tensorflow.
